10 Indian Actors Who Played Real People And Absolutely Nailed it

Portraying a real person on screen is one of the most demanding challenges an actor can face. It requires authenticity, emotional depth, physical transformation, and the ability to understand the subject’s life with honesty. These 10 Indian actors delivered performances that went far beyond imitation — they brought real individuals, their hardships, and their legacies alive with powerful truth.

1. Ranbir Kapoor as Sanjay Dutt — Sanju

Click to watch the official trailer on YouTube

Official Trailer

Ranbir Kapoor shocked audiences with his near-perfect transformation into Sanjay Dutt. From the walk and voice to the raw emotional breakdowns, Ranbir captured every chapter of Dutt’s controversial yet inspiring life. His portrayal of Sanjay’s vulnerability, charm, pain, and inner chaos has become a benchmark for biographical acting in modern cinema.

2. Vicky Kaushal as Field Marshal Sam Manekshaw — Sam Bahadur

Click to watch the official trailer on YouTube

Official Trailer

Vicky Kaushal delivered a dignified, brilliantly controlled performance as India’s iconic Field Marshal Sam Manekshaw. He mastered Sam’s trademark wit, calm authority, sharp posture, and unique speaking style. The charm, discipline, and power he brought to the role made the legendary war hero feel real, relatable, and deeply inspiring.

3. Vicky Kaushal as Major Vihaan Shergill — Uri

Click to watch the official trailer on YouTube

Official Trailer

Though inspired by real-life military heroes, Vicky’s character in Uri required military-level physical and emotional preparation. He trained extensively in weapons handling, combat, and fitness to portray a special forces officer with authenticity. His grit, discipline, and emotional strength made the character feel like a tribute to India’s real soldiers.

4. Vicky Kaushal as Chhatrapati Sambhaji Maharaj — Chhava

Click to watch the official trailer on YouTube

Official Trailer

In Chhava, Vicky Kaushal brings to life Sambhaji Maharaj — the courageous Maratha king known for his fierce leadership and unbreakable spirit. Vicky underwent intense sword and horse-riding training and worked on Marathi dialects to portray the warrior authentically. His performance captures Sambhaji’s royal aura, rage, loyalty, and painful sacrifices, making it one of his strongest roles yet.

5. Sushant Singh Rajput as M. S. Dhoni — MS Dhoni: The Untold Story

Click to watch the official trailer on YouTube

Official Trailer

Sushant didn’t just play Dhoni — he became him. He trained for months to replicate Dhoni’s cricketing style, including the legendary helicopter shot. Beyond physical accuracy, Sushant captured Dhoni’s calmness, struggles, ambitions, and emotional journey with grace. This remains one of the most loved and iconic biographical performances in Indian cinema.

6. Priyanka Chopra as Mary Kom — Mary Kom

Click to watch the official trailer on YouTube

Official Trailer

Priyanka Chopra underwent months of intensive boxing training to portray Olympic medalist Mary Kom. She captured the athlete’s fiery determination, struggles as a mother, and relentless spirit. Her powerful performance helped bring Mary Kom’s story into the national spotlight, inspiring countless women across India.

7. Farhan Akhtar as Milkha Singh — Bhaag Milkha Bhaag

Click to watch the official trailer on YouTube

Official Trailer

Farhan Akhtar’s physical and emotional transformation into Milkha Singh set a new standard for commitment in biographical roles. He trained like a professional athlete for months and portrayed Milkha’s traumatic childhood, discipline, ambition, and emotional journey with deep sincerity. The performance remains a landmark in Indian cinema.

8. Aamir Khan as Mahavir Singh Phogat — Dangal

Click to watch the official trailer on YouTube

Official Trailer

Aamir Khan delivered a masterclass in transformation by gaining and losing huge amounts of weight to portray Mahavir Singh Phogat at different stages of life. His Haryanvi accent, rural mannerisms, and emotional portrayal of a strict-yet-loving father made the film authentic and powerful. Dangal is widely praised for Aamir’s unmatched dedication.

9. Mammootty as Dr. B. R. Ambedkar — Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar

Click to watch the official trailer on YouTube

Official Trailer

Mammootty portrayed Dr. Ambedkar with immense dignity, calm intensity, and deep respect. He captured the leader’s intellectual strength, battles against discrimination, and unwavering determination. The performance required maturity and emotional precision — Mammootty exceeded every expectation.

10. Sivakarthikeyan as Major Mukund Varadarajan — Amaran

Click to watch the official trailer on YouTube

Official Trailer

Sivakarthikeyan delivered one of the most sincere performances of his career as Major Mukund Varadarajan, the Ashoka Chakra awardee. He trained physically, emotionally, and technically to portray the martyr with respect and authenticity. His performance captures the courage, compassion, and sacrifice of a true national hero.

11. Diljit Dosanjh in Real-Life-Based Roles — Punjab 1984, Chamkila

Click to watch the official trailer on YouTube

Official Trailer

Diljit Dosanjh has mastered roles inspired by real people and real events. In Punjab 1984 and Chamkila, he delivers emotionally rich and painfully honest portrayals, capturing innocence, vulnerability, and raw intensity. His natural acting style makes his real-life-based characters feel deeply personal and relatable.

12. Kamal Haasan as Velu Nayakan — Nayakan

Click to watch the official trailer on YouTube

Official Trailer

Kamal Haasan’s iconic performance as Velu Nayakan — inspired by real Mumbai underworld figure Varadarajan Mudaliar — is widely regarded as one of the greatest performances in Indian cinema. Kamal flawlessly portrayed the character across different ages, capturing the transformation from a vulnerable young boy to a powerful yet emotionally tormented don. His subtle expressions, detailed body language, and emotional depth made Velu Nayakan feel real, human, and unforgettable. The role remains a timeless example of world-class acting.
